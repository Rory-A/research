Incoming MSc Artificial Intelligence, School of Informatics, University of Edinburgh (September 2026).

I am interested in generative models, and in particular how we might extract what is meaningful from an artwork and use it to condition what a model generates. My current work looks at what frozen vision embeddings actually capture about artistic style, and whether apparent style understanding survives when artist identity is controlled for.

Before moving into AI research, I spent eight years as a backend software engineer (Kotlin, Java, C#), most recently in a senior engineering role at Lendable.

## Research

**Style or Signature? Artist-Disjoint Evaluation of Style Classification in Frozen Vision Embeddings**
Sole author. Under review at the VISART workshop, ECCV 2026.
The paper argues that the high style-classification accuracy of frozen vision embeddings may be artist recognition rather than genuine style understanding. Holding out whole artists in turn, accuracy drops unevenly across movements, with Surrealism collapsing while Impressionism and Cubism hold up, a pattern consistent across four encoders including a vision-only one.
